The Problem We Solve

Shrinkage Is Costing Your
Foundry Real Money

Every casting with shrinkage porosity is a rejected part, wasted metal, lost time, and a damaged customer relationship. The right sleeve changes all of that.

Shrinkage Porosity
When liquid metal solidifies without adequate feeding, internal voids form in thick sections — causing rejections at final inspection, rework costs, and delayed deliveries.
Metal Wastage
Oversized risers compensate for poor feeding efficiency — locking up expensive liquid metal in feeders that never contribute to the casting. Every extra kilo of riser metal is direct profit lost.
Inconsistent Supply
Variable sleeve quality between batches causes unpredictable results on the foundry floor — forcing metallurgists to constantly re-validate and adjust riser designs, wasting engineering time.

Super Foundrytech sleeves are engineered to eliminate all three problems — simultaneously.

What is the difference between insulating and exothermic sleeves?+

Insulating sleeves reduce heat loss from the riser passively — they act like a thermos flask, keeping the existing heat in the metal for longer. Exothermic sleeves go further: they contain a compound that reacts with the heat of liquid metal and generates additional heat, actively keeping the riser liquid far longer. For lighter castings, insulating is sufficient. For heavy, complex, or high-value castings, exothermic gives superior results.
